The Product

Good Hope Produce sources Peruvian mandarins from certified orchards in Huaral, Lima — one of Peru's primary citrus-producing regions. Four varieties across two overlapping harvest windows give importers flexible supply coverage from April through September, directly addressing Northern Hemisphere off-season gaps.

All production operates under Global G.A.P. and GRASP certification, with full traceability from field to packing house. Minimum Brix ≥ 9.0°, export corrugated cartons in 10 kg and 15 kg grades, cold chain mandatory from pre-cooling through reefer container delivery.

Why Peruvian Mandarins

Four varieties.
One certified
supply partner.

Peruvian mandarins are structurally counter-cyclical to European and US production. Satsuma opens from April, W. Murcott, Tango, and Orri follow through September — covering the precise window when Northern Hemisphere volumes tighten. Good Hope Produce gives procurement teams a certified, traceability-documented partner for that window.

Our orchards in Huaral operate under Global G.A.P. certification with GRASP verification and full MRL compliance to EU standards — the documentation your compliance and sustainability teams require, already in place. Calibers X through 4X in 10 kg cartons, and calibers 1–3 in 15 kg cartons, with ventilated export packaging suitable for palletized sea freight.

Each shipment comes with full post-harvest documentation: shelf life up to 3 weeks at 4°C, cold chain certified, Brix/acidity ratio balanced to export standards with maximum 3% cosmetic defect tolerance. If your annual plan has a supply gap between April and September and you need a certified partner your auditors will approve — the conversation starts here.
